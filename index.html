Our Story

Our Mission

We are a coalition of persons with lived experience of homelessness committed to enhancing our community by building the foundations necessary to support those working to permanently transition out of homelessness.

Our Aim

We use lived experiences of homelessness to bring people together and build healthy relationships needed to support those working to permanently transition out of homelessness. Through capacity building, training, and coordination, we're creating a more compassionate San Antonio—one that responds to unsheltered populations with dignity and effective supportive services.

Street Outreach Leadership

Chris Parker serves as our Street Pastor, leading intervention efforts by building trust through a soft approach via street case management and helping individuals navigate resources and services to begin their transition journey.

Humility

Approaching our work with openness and a willingness to learn from every person we serve.

Respect

Treating every individual with dignity, regardless of their circumstances.

Collaboration

Working together with partners, organizations, and communities to maximize impact.

The Coalition

Stronger Together

The Hope Heals Coalition is 100% comprised of Persons with Lived Experience. We bring together SA Hope Center, All Wellness Center, First Presbyterian Church, Broken Warriors Angels, Life Restored, Mental Health Department, KRL Staff, STCH Ministries, and trained community members to coordinate supportive services and advocacy efforts across San Antonio.

01

Immediate Outreach

Connect individuals facing or experiencing homelessness to the appropriate resources that meet their mental and physical needs, including Traumatic Brain Injury and mental health group meetings as an avenue for outreach and support.

02

Intervention

Building trust by offering a soft approach through street case management and helping individuals navigate resources and services. Creating social networks that help support folks experiencing a mental health crisis.

03

Advocacy

Offer programs to community stakeholders to increase awareness of the needs of the homeless, especially mental health and social support needs. Address citywide policies that create barriers—including streamlining ID recovery procedures.

04

Education

Build a narrative of change using success stories to increase public awareness about the value of advocacy programs among city officials, schools, and community stakeholders. Financial literacy advocacy for youth to foster a culture of resilience.

Our Impact

Every training session, every partnership, and every advocacy effort moves us closer to a San Antonio where everyone is treated with dignity.
